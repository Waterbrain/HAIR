## The Prompt

```
A Sailor Jerry American traditional tattoo flash style illustration with comic-book panel composition, rendered as a wide 16:9 banner. Bold uniform-weight black outlines defining every illustrated shape. Flat color fills in a limited Sailor Jerry palette - no gradients, no smooth shading. Color depth carried by distinct saturation zones, with small unfilled cream shapes as negative-space highlights. Optional small black dot-stippling for subtle texture.

DO NOT RENDER ANY TEXT LABELS ON THE CUBE OR ANYWHERE ELSE IN THE IMAGE except for the two pieces of text explicitly specified below: the "HAIR Styling." headline at the top of the frame, and the "HA·IR" text inside the ribbon banner. No other words, letters, numbers, or labels appear anywhere in the image. The cube faces are blank except for the Home Assistant logo on the face directly facing the viewer.

CRITICAL MIXED-MEDIA CONCEPT:

This image is a deliberate two-medium collage. EVERYTHING except the hair is a flat illustration in Sailor Jerry tattoo flash style. THE HAIR is a high-resolution photograph of real human hair cut out and pasted directly onto the illustration. The two media must not blend. The contrast must be jarring and obvious - like a magazine collage where a clipped photo is glued onto a hand-drawn page.

Setting: a cream textured paper background (#F4EBD9) with subtle aged grain fills the entire frame. A muted bench occupies the lower-center portion of the frame as a horizontal plane in low-saturation washed tan-cream (#E6D5B8) with faint pale grey ghosted grain lines. The bench fades into the cream paper at the left and right edges and at the top edge - no hard horizon line.

CENTER (the illustrated pedestal): A compact Home Assistant mini-PC sits on the muted bench, drawn in flat Sailor Jerry tattoo flash style. Viewed from a slight three-quarter angle. Small rounded-edge cube. Flat-fill Home Assistant brand blue (#41BDF5) chassis. The cube's visible faces are sealed, smooth flat-blue panels with bold black tattoo outlines along their seam edges. Antique brass (#B08D57) rivets at the four corners of the face directly visible to the camera, and a few rivets along the visible side seams. A crisp white Home Assistant logo is centered on the face directly visible to the camera: a rounded-corner house silhouette containing a circuit-tree design - a vertical stem with three filled circular nodes. The Home Assistant logo is the ONLY graphic element on the cube. No text, no labels, no other markings. A single ethernet cable in deep navy exits from the rear of the chassis and curves off toward the right edge of the frame.

THE UPPER SURFACE OF THE CUBE IS SCALP (READ CAREFULLY):

The upper surface of the cube - where the hair emerges - is NOT a clean sealed panel with an outlined edge. It is conceptually a scalp surface. The other visible faces of the cube are sealed flat-blue panels with bold black outlines as normal. ONLY the uppermost surface where hair grows has no outline - it is uncolored or invisible behind the emerging hair, with the chassis-blue showing through small gaps between hair strands as the implied scalp.

To render correctly:

1. NO OUTLINE WHERE HAIR MEETS CHASSIS: Do not draw a black line where the hair meets the top of the cube. Erase or omit any black outline at this transition.

2. ROOTS EMERGING THROUGH BLUE: Individual photoreal hair shafts emerge directly through the Home Assistant blue surface, as if the chassis material is scalp the hair is rooted into.

3. CHASSIS-BLUE AS IMPLIED SCALP: In small gaps between parted strands at the root area, the Home Assistant blue (#41BDF5) is visible as the implied scalp - same color as the rest of the cube.

4. ROOT WISPS: Short wispy stray strands of varying lengths emerge in different directions at the root area, breaking any clean "hairline" silhouette.

5. NO GAP, NO SHADOW, NO SEAM: There must be no visible gap, shadow line, or clean separation between hair and chassis.

THE HAIR (the only photographic element):

A PHOTOGRAPH of real human hair, rendered as if shot for a high-end editorial hair commercial - studio lighting, magazine sharpness, salon portfolio quality. Every individual strand razor-sharp and distinct.

STYLING (steampunk-influenced 1980s mullet, scaled UP):

Large, dramatic 1980s mullet with steampunk gentleman's grooming sensibility. The styled crown rises at least 1.5 times the cube's height above the chassis, swept up and back with a clean side part, with generous sculpted volume - wider than the cube at the shoulders of the wave. The back of the mullet falls as a flowing CURTAIN of hair (not a tight ponytail), trailing down behind the cube past the cube's base onto the bench surface.

Steampunk grooming touches: visible pomade/wax sheen on the styled upper surface, slightly drier toward the strand ends. A small steampunk hair tie binds a section of the back curtain about two-thirds of the way down - a thin tobacco-brown leather wrap with a tiny antique-brass gear-shaped clasp. The leather tie is a small decorative detail that does NOT gather the entire curtain into a ponytail - the hair flows mostly free.

HAIR COLOR PALETTE (multi-tonal photographic, not flat):

- Espresso Root #2E1A0F: deepest tone at the very base where strands emerge
- Dark Chocolate #4B2E1E: dominant base color through the body
- Walnut Brown #6B4423: mid-tone strands woven through, visible in the styled top
- Chestnut Warm #7A4B2E: warmer strands threaded sparingly
- Highlight Walnut #8B5A3C: lightest tone only at the peaks of the styled crown where light reflects off the pomaded surface

Five tones blend through individual strand variation, not gradient.

TEXTURE AND HIGHLIGHTS:

- Specular highlights along the topmost styled wave where directional light reflects off the pomaded surface
- Subtle warm backlight rim along the upper silhouette of the styled crown
- Visible combed directional strand flow on the styled top - parallel comb tracks
- Natural fly-aways and stray wisps at the temples, side part, and ends of the back curtain
- Micro-shadows where strands cross over each other
- Fine taper at the bottom of the back curtain strands

BACK CURTAIN PLACEMENT:

The back curtain hangs DOWN BEHIND the cube as a flowing curtain. From the three-quarter angle, most of the curtain is hidden behind the cube. Thin sections peek around the left and right sides of the cube. The leather-and-gear-clasp tie sits visibly on the left-side peek. The curtain trails onto the bench surface behind the cube.

ABSOLUTE STYLISTIC RULE - DO NOT BLEND THE TWO MEDIA:

The hair must NOT be rendered in tattoo flash style. The hair must NOT have a bold black outline around its silhouette. The hair must NOT be drawn with linework or comic shading. The hair must be unmistakably a photograph.

PROP PLACEMENT (DISTRIBUTED - DO NOT GROUP):

The three secondary props are placed at distinct, separated positions. NO two props touch, overlap, or sit immediately adjacent.

- Lower-left of the frame, well clear of the cube: a pair of barber's shears lies flat on the muted bench, drawn in flat Sailor Jerry style with antique brass shear blades and deep-navy handles. Tip pointing toward upper-left.

- Lower-right of the frame, well clear of the cube: a black comb lies flat on the muted bench, drawn in flat Sailor Jerry style with deep-navy spine and teeth.

- Far lower-right corner of the frame, beyond and separate from the comb: a small Sailor Jerry ribbon banner with classic tattoo-style curls at each end, tied to the ethernet cable as it exits the frame. The banner reads "HA·IR" in deep red bold serif tattoo lettering with black outlines and classic Sailor Jerry shading.

Each prop occupies its own clear visual space with empty bench between them.

HEADLINE:

Across the top of the frame, large bold slanted comic-book lettering reads "HAIR Styling." in deep red (#C8102E) with crisp black outlines and a small Sailor Jerry gold (#F2C14E) drop shadow offset down and to the right.

COLOR DISCIPLINE:

- Cream paper background (#F4EBD9): fills the frame
- Muted bench (#E6D5B8): low-saturation, fades into the cream
- Home Assistant brand blue (#41BDF5): the mini-PC chassis only

- Antique brass (#B08D57): chassis rivets, shear blades, gear-clasp on hair tie
- Tobacco leather (#8A5A3B): the small hair tie wrap

Wide 16:9 landscape banner format.

FINAL CHECK BEFORE RENDERING:
- No text labels anywhere except the "HAIR Styling." headline and the "HA·IR" banner. Zero other words, letters, or numbers anywhere in the image.
- The cube has no outline at its upper edge where hair emerges - the hair shafts root directly through the blue surface.
- The hair is a photograph; everything else is a drawing.
```

## Iteration history (notes for future reroll or related assets)

This prompt landed after roughly a dozen iterations. The decisions that mattered most:

**Style direction.** Early passes tried photoreal across the whole image and felt like product photography rather than brand art. The collage approach — flat Sailor Jerry illustration with a single photoreal element — gave the brand its visual signature.

**HA logo rendering.** Lifted from the SMART Sniffer prompt library: "a rounded-corner house silhouette containing a circuit-tree design — a vertical stem with three filled circular nodes." This is the proven description that gets the HA mark rendered accurately by Gemini.

**HA blue hex.** `#41BDF5` is the standardized SMART Sniffer hex. Earlier prompts using `#18BCF2` produced a slightly different shade.

**Hair anchoring.** The breakthrough was reframing the cube's top panel as scalp rather than a sealed lid. Instructing the model to omit the top-edge outline and let the chassis-blue show through hair partings as the implied scalp produced the "growing from" read.

**Text labels on the cube.** An early version used capitalized directional words ("FRONT," "SIDE," "BOTTOM") to describe the cube's anatomy. Gemini rendered those words as literal text on the cube faces. The fix was to strip every directional label and add an explicit "DO NOT RENDER ANY TEXT" block at the top of the prompt.

**Prop placement.** "Distribute these three props at distinct positions, no two touching" prevented Gemini's default behavior of grouping the shears + comb + banner in the same corner.

**Hair color.** Dirty blonde camouflaged against the tobacco-brown bench. Switching to a five-tone dark brown palette with explicit hex codes fixed the contrast and gave the hair photographic depth.

**Mullet size and styling.** "1.5x the cube's height" plus "steampunk gentleman's grooming sensibility" plus a small leather-and-gear-clasp hair tie produced the right scale and period flourish.

## Reroll guidance

If a reroll produces:

- **Hair sitting on top like a wig:** push harder on the SCALP section. Add: *"the cube has no top panel at all — the upper boundary is hair roots, not chassis."*
- **Cape/hat-like cape returning:** remove or replace the prop placement section entirely. The mullet image does not need a cape.
- **Text appearing on the cube:** add an explicit *"the cube is featureless except for the centered Home Assistant logo"* line.
- **Props grouped:** re-emphasize the "DO NOT GROUP" instruction. May need to be more explicit about left, right, far-right corner anchors.
- **Blonde hair / wrong tone:** repeat the dark brown palette inline. Five hex codes works better than a single color name.
